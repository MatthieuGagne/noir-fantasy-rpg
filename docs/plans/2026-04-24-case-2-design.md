# Case 2: The Document Case (working title)

## Overview

Reid and Karim follow Crane's first Vesper lead — her last known address in the Heights. The Grays have already swept it. What remains are ley terminal output logs Vesper left intentionally. Simultaneously, a Gold Coat operation is running a silent hunt across two districts for a fugitive Black Mage pulling classified calibration records. Both threads converge at the Four Winds Bar. Margot joins. Kurtz is introduced, unrecognized.

---

## Beat Map

### BEAT 1 — THE FIRST LEAD (Four Winds Bar)

Crane meets Reid. Off the record. He gives Reid Vesper's last known address in the Heights — an apartment maintained under a false name, a thread only Crane knew about. He doesn't know what's there. He knows she left something behind intentionally.

*[Story beat. Opens the Vesper investigation.]*

---

### BEAT 2 — THE ADDRESS (Heights — Vesper's Apartment)

Reid and Karim investigate. Investigation mode — search the apartment, read the space. Vesper stripped it deliberately but left one thing: ley terminal output logs, printed and annotated in her hand. Dense technical notation. Karim can't fully parse it. Reid can't either. Someone with Academy formation would know what they're looking at.

The apartment has been swept — Bureau Gray footprints, recent. Someone got here first and missed the logs.

*[Investigation mode: searching for evidence, reading scenes.]*

---

### BEAT 3 — THE SWEEP (Heights — Stairwell / Street)

The Grays are still in the building. Not here for Reid — not yet — but his presence triggers assessment. A Gray team, professional, Bureau-trained. And at the edge of the operation: a figure in a gold coat, observing from the far end of the corridor. Reid sees the team. Sees the coat. Files it and moves.

The Gray team moves to intercept. Hot Zone.

*[ATB combat: Reid + Karim vs. Gray team. First direct conflict with Bureau enforcement. The gold coat withdraws before Reid can close.]*

---

### BEAT 4 — THE THREAD (Sprawl — transit)

Moving fast. Karim reads the apartment logs in fragments — he recognizes the pattern. Entity-contact scarring isn't just in bodies. Vesper was tracking the same accumulation in the ley architecture's output records. Someone else has been building a parallel case from the calibration records angle.

He gives Reid a name: *"She called herself Margot. Academy-trained. She came to my clinic once, asking about calibrated patients I'd treated. Left quickly. I don't have a file on her."*

One of Karim's patients — a calibrated Grade III, vanished six months ago — fits the pattern in Vesper's logs exactly.

*[Story/exploration beat. Establishes the parallel investigation. Karim's patient becomes the lead.]*

---

### BEAT 5 — THE SAFEHOUSE TRAIL (Sprawl — two locations)

Reid traces Margot's movement through Sprawl channels. Two safehouses in the last month. The second: empty, recently vacated, evidence of sustained research work. Printouts. Academy-trained notation on every page. The Bureau got here first — the space was tossed. They were looking for the document case. They didn't find it.

*[Exploration/investigation: reading the tossed safehouse, understanding what the Grays are hunting.]*

---

### BEAT 6 — THE CORDON (Sprawl — street level)

The Gray operation has widened into a sweep. Multiple encounters, escalating — the Grays operating outside their Heights jurisdiction, improvising, which makes them less predictable. During the fight: a ley terminal in this block pulses once, wrong. Karim notices.

*[ATB combat: Reid + Karim vs. Gray sweep teams. Multi-encounter escalation.]*

---

### BEAT 7 — THE BAR (Four Winds — Margot arrives)

They make it back to the Four Winds. Reid is processing: the apartment logs, Karim's patient, a Gold Coat running a two-district operation — this is bigger than a fugitive jailbreaker.

Margot sits down beside him. Not across. The document case is open on the table before she says a word.

*"You helped Vesper walk. I need to know why she was worth the cost. Because I think whatever she found is why these people are gone."*

The conversation: Reid asks his questions. She gives the data — the disappearance cohort, the output surplus, the consumption spikes. Karim recognizes his patient in her records. Three people looking at the same machine from three different angles.

The Gold Coat's team finds the bar.

*[Story beat — recruitment scene — interrupted by Hot Zone.]*

---

### BEAT 8 — THE BAR FIGHT (Four Winds — Hot Zone)

The same gold coat from Beat 3 — closer now. This time it's not assessment. The document case is on the table and they want it.

Margot and Reid fight together — forced party. She fights with what the Academy gave her: precise, controlled, Grade IV technique. When her limit break lands, it's something the Academy didn't teach — still learning its own shape. Raw.

The Gold Coat observes from the back of the room. Does not engage. Reid sees the face in the crowd — a scar through the left brow, catching the bar light. He files it. Doesn't place it. The gold coat is wrong, the rank is wrong, twenty years is a long time. The coat withdraws when the fight turns.

*[ATB combat: Reid + Karim + Margot (guest) vs. Gray enforcement team. First look at Margot's combat style.]*

---

### BEAT 9 — THE DECISION (Four Winds — aftermath)

Margot could leave. She has what she came for — Reid is pursuing Vesper, the investigation is converging on the same machine she's been mapping.

She doesn't.

*"The Grays just ran a sweep across two Sprawl districts for a fugitive jailbreaker with a document case. That's not normal resourcing. Whatever I found — you need to understand it before they get it back. And I need to understand what Vesper found. We're working the same case."*

She's in.

*[Story beat — Margot joins the party.]*

## Characters

### Margot — Black Mage
See `CHARACTERS.md` for full entry.

Joins at Beat 9. Brings the document case: calibration records, disappearance cohort data, output surplus accounting that doesn't close. Grade IV Bureau insider knowledge — she understands the system's language from inside the institution. ## Story Beats & Themes

**Two investigations, one machine.** Reid is following Vesper forward. Margot has been following the calibration program's damage backward. They meet in the middle — the same machine, different entry points.

**The Gold Coat as wrongness.** In a world of dark nocturnal uniforms, a full gold coat is aggressive, impossible. The Bureau defers to it without knowing why. That deference is the horror made visible.

**Necessity makes allies again.** Reid and Margot fight together before either chooses it — the same dynamic as Reid and Iris in the opening, Reid and Iris again in Case 1. The pattern is structural. Alliances in this city form under fire.

**Karim's role expands.** He is the thread connecting both investigations — he treated calibrated practitioners (Margot's data), he treated Casimir (entity-contact scarring), he named Vesper (the Vesper case). He is the party's institutional memory of the machine's damage.

---

## Connections & Foreshadowing

- **Kurtz's gold coat** — first appearance of a WARDEN designation in the physical world. The player doesn't know what it means yet. Karim notices the ley terminal pulse during Beat 6. The two things are connected.
- **Margot's document case** — the ledger of the disappearance cohort will become a key investigation tool. The gaps in her records (cases she can't access) are what the party will fill across Act 1.
- **Vesper's apartment logs** — Margot can read them. What they contain drives the next thread.
- **The face Reid files** — Kurtz, unrecognized, at the Four Winds. A gold coat, a scar through the left brow. Reid put that scar there the night Vesper walked. When the recognition comes later — the distance finally closing, the scar finally placing the face — it reframes everything in Case 2 retroactively. The man he's been fighting through Gray teams is the peer he marked twenty years ago. The gold coat is what happened to that scar in the time since.
