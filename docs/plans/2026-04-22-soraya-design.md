# Character Design: Soraya (Hacker)

**Session date:** 2026-04-22

---

## Soraya — Hacker

**Class:** Hacker
**Bureau status:** No file — always outside the system. Actively erases Grid activity logs so the Bureau knows someone is going deep but not who or how far. She keeps it that way.

### Background
Iranian/Persian. Mid-to-late 50s. Has been in the deep Grid longer than most of the party has been alive. Self-taught — no Academy, no Bureau formation, no licensed framework shaping what she thought she was doing when she found what's down there.

She was in the deep Grid when it changed — when the entity's presence became felt at depth. Rather than pulling back, she went deeper. Methodically, carefully, over years and then decades. She has mapped the entity's information layer with more precision than anyone alive. She knows its shape without knowing its name. When Reid's investigation starts uncovering what's beneath NOX, she already has the map. She's been waiting for someone to ask the right questions.

### Bureau Status — The Erasure
She was never licensed or registered. What she erases are Grid activity logs — the passive signatures the Bureau's ley terminal network records whenever someone accesses the Grid at depth. Every deep dive leaves a trace. She's been systematically cleaning those traces for decades. The Bureau knows someone is going very deep. They don't know who. She intends to keep it that way.

### Mechanic
Hacks enemy cyberware and sigils to malfunction and backfire. Hacked enemies attack their own allies. Misfired sigils damage their casters. Disrupted systems cascade into adjacent systems. Deals almost no direct damage — the most indirect fighter in the party. The Grid is the vector; the enemy's own infrastructure is the weapon.

Distinct from Kos (Occultist / Blue Mage): Kos absorbs what is done to him and turns it outward. Soraya turns the enemy's systems against themselves. Kos is passive acquisition; Soraya is active redirection.

### The Grid-Glow
Decades of neural interface have left a mark — a faint cold blue glow visible in her iris in certain light. She wears it openly. She's been outside the Bureau's system her whole life and has nothing to hide it from. It's the one tell she can't erase. She's made peace with it.

What is known: she has spent decades alone with knowledge nobody else has. She wonders if the deep Grid exposure changed her — not like Kos, but subtly. She doesn't examine that question. The map is what she has. She tends the map.

### Appearance
Iranian/Persian, mid-to-late 50s, warm olive skin. Electric blue hair, long and loose — the only blue hair in the cast, cold and digital against warm skin. Sharp, precise features. Dark eyes with a faint Grid-glow in the iris, worn openly. Lean, upright, deliberate posture. A long white fitted tech coat — the only white clothing in the cast — with interface ports integrated at wrist and temple, glowing cold blue when she works. Dominant color: **white / cold blue accents**.

**16-bit read:** The only white coat in a cast of dark clothing — reads against every background and every party member. Electric blue long hair on warm olive skin is the sharpest warm/cool contrast in the cast. The Grid-glow is the accent in portrait close-ups.
